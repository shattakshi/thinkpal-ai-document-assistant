Retrieval-Augmented Generation, often called RAG, is a pattern where a language model does not answer only from its internal parameters. 
Instead, it first retrieves relevant pieces of text from an external knowledge base, such as a vector database, and then uses those texts as additional context for the answer.

The basic flow is: a user asks a question, we convert that question into an embedding, and then we use that embedding to search a vector store for similar chunks of text. 
The retrieved chunks are formatted into a context string, which is passed along with the question into the large language model. 
Because the model sees real documents in the prompt, its answer is more grounded and easier to trace back to sources.

RAG is useful whenever we want the model to stay up to date, use private data, or reduce hallucinations. 
Instead of fine-tuning a model for every change in the knowledge base, we only update the documents and their embeddings in the vector database.
