Vector embeddings are numerical representations of text. 
The goal of an embedding model is to map semantically similar pieces of text to vectors that are close to each other in a high dimensional space. 
In a RAG system, embeddings are used both for documents and for user questions.

The typical workflow is: we split documents into chunks, clean the text, and send each chunk to an embedding model. 
The output vectors are stored in a vector database like Chroma or FAISS together with metadata such as source file and page number. 
Later, when a user sends a query, we embed the query and perform a similarity search against those stored vectors.

Cosine similarity or dot product is often used to measure how close two vectors are. 
Higher similarity scores indicate that the document chunk is more relevant to the query. 
Good embeddings and a well-tuned similarity search are critical for a high quality retriever.
